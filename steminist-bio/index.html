Dr. Ellen Ochoa

Ellen Ochoa is a former astronaut who was the first Hispanic woman to travel into space in 1993. Ochoa earned a bachelor’s degree in physics from San Diego State University and a master’s degree and doctorate in electrical engineering from Stanford University. Ochoa has been recognized with NASA’s highest award, the Distinguished Service Medal, and the Presidential Distinguished Rank Award for senior executives in the federal government. She later served as director of NASA’s Johnson Space Center from 2013 to 2018.
